Nourish Your Body, Clear Your Mind.

The Mission Statement

"Addressing concerns in youth regarding malnutrition... We focus primarily on diet, while also exploring exercise and other health factors."

India faces a dual challenge: Undernutrition (lack of food) and Overnutrition (junk food/obesity). From rural tribal groups to urban workers relying on processed snacks, our nutrition is in crisis.

Why This Matters

52%

of adolescent girls are chronically undernourished.

101st

India's rank on the Global Hunger Index (out of 116).

1 in 7

Indians suffer from mental health issues linked to diet.

Critical Health Concerns

It isn't just about being hungry. It's about what we are eating and what it does to our bodies.

Deficiency

Anemia

What: Low red blood cells/iron causing fatigue.

Stat: 58.5% of children under five and 53% of women are anemic.

Fix it with green leafy veggies & beans!

Systemic Issue

Malnutrition (Stunting)

What: Children facing long-term physical and cognitive challenges due to lack of food.

Stat: 31% of children under five in Odisha are stunted.

Lifestyle Disease

Non-Communicable Diseases

What: Diabetes, Heart Disease, Obesity caused by processed diets.

Risk: India is projected to have the highest number of diabetes cases by 2045.

Well-being

Mental Health Connection

Your brain needs fuel! Poor diets weaken memory & focus. Eating whole grains, fruits, and Omega-3s reduces risks of depression and anxiety.

Dispelling Misconceptions

MYTH: "Nutritional diseases only affect the underprivileged."

THE REALITY

Nutritional imbalance is persistent amongst the middle and higher class as well. This is driven by Unhealthy Affluence (access to fast food), Sedentary Urban Lifestyles, and High Stress environments. Wealth does not guarantee health if the dietary choices are poor.

Common Pseudoscience Myths

MYTH: "Detox diets cleanse your body of toxins."
FACT

Your body has its own highly efficient detox system: the liver and kidneys. No juice cleanse or tea can do what your organs already do.

MYTH: "Carbohydrates make you fat."
FACT

Carbs are the body's primary energy source. The issue is refined carbs. Complex carbs like whole grains are essential.

MYTH: "Brown sugar is healthier than white sugar."
FACT

Brown sugar is simply white sugar with molasses. To your body, sugar is sugar, and both spike insulin levels equally.

MYTH: "Supplements can replace real food."
FACT

Whole foods provide a synergy of fiber, antioxidants, and micronutrients that pills cannot replicate.

The Unjunked Action Plan

We need community-driven innovation. Here is what we propose:

01.

Nutritious Office Lunches

Stop the microwave meals! Offices should partner with suppliers for fresh, balanced lunches and use cafeteria boards to educate employees on why it matters.

02.

Better School Meal Plans

School food is often chosen for convenience, not health. We must overhaul school menus to ensure options are actually nutritious, not just filling.

03.

Advertise "Real" Food

You see ads for chips and instant noodles everywhere. We need aggressive advertising for healthy, whole foods to influence society's choices.

04.

Accessible Food Delis

Create "Unjunked Delis" providing nutritious, cooked food at fair prices for busy urban workers and subsidized costs for rural families.

Our Impact Journey

Phase 1: Research & Prep

Project Initiation

We conducted a project survey to gain insights into the general public's eating habits, designed awareness posters, and compiled our initial research findings.

Phase 2: Awareness

Spreading the Word

We pasted our designed posters around the school campus to bring immediate awareness to the issue of malnutrition and junk food consumption.

Phase 3: Solution Design

Recipe Development

We researched commonly consumed unhealthy foods and developed "Unjunked" recipe cards for delicious, healthy alternatives, testing them to ensure quality.

Phase 4: Workshop Execution

Grade 6 Healthy Eating Activity

We successfully conducted a healthy eating workshop with the Grade 6 class. This involved interactive activities to teach younger students about nutritious choices in a fun, engaging way.

Phase 5: Community Outreach

Events & Stalls

We managed stalls at the Cambridge Fest and Unwind events. We spoke directly with parents and students about the topic, handing out our healthy eating brochures and recipe cards to the wider community.
